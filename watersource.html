Understanding Water Sources

An educational guide to the Earth's primary water sources

Rivers

Rivers are flowing bodies of freshwater that provide vital water for agriculture, drinking, and industry. They play a critical role in ecosystems and help transport nutrients across regions.

Read More

Lakes

Lakes are large bodies of still water found in depressions. They store freshwater and are often used for recreation, fishing, and water supply. Lakes also serve as important habitats for wildlife.

Read More

Groundwater

Groundwater is water found underground in aquifers and soil. It supplies drinking water for billions of people and supports agricultural and industrial needs. It's accessed through wells and natural springs.

Read More

Oceans

Oceans contain around 97% of the Earth's water. Though salty, they support marine life, regulate climate, and are potential sources for desalinated drinking water.
