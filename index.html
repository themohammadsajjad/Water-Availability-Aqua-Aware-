Water Sources

Rainwater Harvesting

Rainwater harvesting (RWH) is the practice of collecting and storing rainwater for later use. It's a water conservation method that can help reduce the demand on groundwater and surface water, and improve water quality.

View more

Groundwater

Groundwater is water that is stored underground in the soil or in the cracks and pores of rocks. It is a renewable resource that is formed when rain or other precipitation seeps into the ground and fills the spaces in the soil and rocks.

View more

Surface Water

Surface water is any water that is found on the surface of the Earth, including water in rivers, lakes, streams, oceans, seas, and wetlands. It is a vital part of the water cycle and is essential for life on Earth.

Water Conservation

What Is Water Conservation?
Water conservation is the practice of using water efficiently to reduce unnecessary water usage. According to Fresh Water Watch, water conservation is important because fresh clean water is a limited resource, as well as a costly one. As a homeowner, you’re probably already well aware of the financial costs of inefficient water use. Conservation of this natural resource is critical for the environment — and our wallets.
